31  * @defgroup linked_list Linked Lists
32  * @brief a modified doubly-linked list implementation from the linux kernel
33  *
34  *
35  * This is a (slightly modified) doubly-linked list implementation from the
36  * Linux kernel. An easy to understand explanation of
37  * how it works and is used can be found here
38  * http://kernelnewbies.org/FAQ/LinkedLists
39  *
40  * Be smart
41  * -----
42  * It is a valid to criticise linked lists as generally performing badly
43  * if you traverse their entries at high frequency rather than just
44  * manipulating them. This can be especially true (and has been demonstrated
45  * numerous times) for implementations like std:list.
46  *
47  * Please consider the following though: linked lists are not inherently
48  * slow because of how they work algorithmically (*the opposite is true*),
49  * but rather because how their cache hit (or miss) rate is in
50  * configurations where entries are randomly scattered in memory rather
51  * than laid out in one big chunk.
52  *
53  * Be smart. Don't do that. Allocate a pool in a __single chunk__ and enjoy the
54  * cache performance. Do not use larger chunks than the page size of your
55  * platform if you have MMU support. If you need more than that, you probably
56  * need to redesign your program anyways.
57  *
58  * This does of course not apply as long as you do access your lists only
59  * at slow rates (i.e. in the order of several tens of ms) or if performance
60  * is not at all critial.
61  *
62  */
